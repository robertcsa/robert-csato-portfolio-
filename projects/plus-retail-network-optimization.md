## 1. Overview

This document describes the large-scale retail logistics optimisation project carried out for **Plus Warenhandelsgesellschaft mbH**, one of Germany’s major discount retailers during the early 2000s.

The project included:

- full analysis of the regional distribution network,  
- in-depth evaluation of **~430 retail stores**,  
- on-site assessment of **store-level buffer capacities**,  
- optimisation of the **hub-and-spoke supply chain**,  
- and management of the **restart of a regional distribution centre** in Kempten after a logistics disruption.

This work formed the foundation for significant improvements in distribution efficiency and inventory availability for Plus stores across NRW and the southern regions.

---

## 2. Business Context

Plus operated a nationwide retail network with:

- decentralised stores of varying sizes,  
- regionally distributed central warehouses,  
- a traditional hub-and-spoke distribution system,  
- daily or alternate-day replenishment cycles,  
- retailer-specific delivery time windows,  
- and strict cost-efficiency expectations.

Challenges included:

- uneven store buffer capacities,  
- demand fluctuations,  
- limited storage in urban locations,  
- seasonal turnover spikes,  
- and high fleet utilisation requirements.

## 3. Project Scope

The optimisation project covered multiple layers:

### **3.1. Store-Level Field Analysis**
Over a period of approx. **two months**, I personally visited around **350 out of 430 stores** to collect:

- buffer stock capacity  
- dry and chilled storage measurements  
- backroom layout  
- receiving dock limitations  
- forklift/pallet jack manoeuvrability  
- historical delivery punctuality  
- daily/weekly stocking patterns  
- bottlenecks and manual handling patterns  

### **3.2. Warehouse—Store Synchronisation**
Key flows were analysed:

- delivery frequency  
- batch sizes  
- picking sequences  
- routing distances  
- driver scheduling  
- service level availability  
- store planogram impact  

### **3.3. Hub-and-Spoke Network Evaluation**
Evaluated structural elements:

- warehouse throughput  
- truck turnaround time  
- dock utilisation  
- cross-docking efficiency  
- regional route clustering  

### **3.4. Warehouse Restart Leadership**
Following a transport sector strike and resulting operational standstill, I was assigned to:

- support the coordination of the warehouse restoration,  
- manage task forces on-site,  
- stabilise inbound/outbound flows,  
- support team leads,  
- ensure rapid return to regular service levels.

## 4. Key Findings

### **4.1. Store Buffer Capacity Variability**
Ranged from extremely limited (urban micro-stores) to sufficient (suburban or rural stores).  
Required dynamic rescheduling of delivery frequency and batch sizes.

### **4.2. Warehouse Picking & Loading Mismatch**
Inconsistent sequencing created delays and congestion at stores.  
Proposed solution: cluster-optimised pick routes.

### **4.3. Route Planning Suboptimalities**
Observed:
- overlapping truck routes  
- underutilised backhaul opportunities  
- peak-time congestion  

### **4.4. High Seasonal Sensitivity**
Holiday season demand required adaptive delivery windows and flexible fleet allocation.

### **4.5. Warehouse Restart Challenges**
Initial conditions:
- large backlog of goods  
- mixed pallets  
- ad-hoc manual reorganisation  
- missing prioritisation sequence  
Resolved through structured triage and priority grouping.

---

## 5. Solutions Implemented

### **5.1. Route Clustering & Scheduling Optimisation**
- reorganised delivery territories  
- introduced cluster-based route cycles  
- improved fuel efficiency and driver utilisation  

### **5.2. Dynamic Buffer-Based Delivery Frequency**
Stores were grouped by:
- buffer size  
- average floor-space velocity  
- category sensitivity  

Result:
- reduced stock-outs  
- stabilised store operations  

### **5.3. Warehouse Throughput Enhancement**
Implemented:
- priority lanes  
- cross-dock acceleration  
- revised picking windows  
- earlier cut-off times for congested stores  

### **5.4. Structured Warehouse Restart (Crisis Response)**
Key steps:
1. triage of inbound pallets  
2. dynamic labour division  
3. quick access reorganisation  
4. operational relaunch within tight deadlines  

### **5.5. KPI Framework Introduced**
Defined and monitored:
- fill rate  
- on-time delivery  
- route utilisation  
- warehouse throughput  
- stock availability
