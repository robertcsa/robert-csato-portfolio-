## 2. Retail & Operational Context

COOP (and the related Plus Élelmiszerdiszkont stores in Hungary) operated:

- discount-format grocery stores  
- daily replenishment cycles  
- small backroom areas  
- high SKU turnover  
- temperature-controlled product categories  
- strict expiration and rotation rules  
- labour-constrained operations

Operational reliability required:

- rapid decision-making  
- accurate staff planning  
- real-time problem solving  
- direct coordination with regional logistics hubs

## 3. Key Responsibilities

### **3.1. Personnel & Shift Management**
- scheduling multi-shift store teams  
- ensuring optimal staffing during peak hours  
- managing recruitment and on-site training  
- supervising cashiers, shelf stockers, and backroom workers  

### **3.2. Store Logistics Coordination**
- receiving daily and weekly deliveries  
- coordinating unloading and backroom allocation  
- organising chilled and frozen product handling  
- maintaining FIFO and FEFO rules  
- resolving loading dock conflicts  

### **3.3. Inventory Management**
- daily inventory checks  
- identification of shrinkage and loss sources  
- analysing stock imbalances  
- coordinating with distribution centres for replenishment  

### **3.4. Merchandising & Category Layout**
- planogram execution  
- promotional material placement  
- seasonal product rollouts  
- ensuring on-shelf availability across key SKUs  

### **3.5. Store Operations Control**
- cash management  
- shift-level financial reconciliation  
- customer service issue resolution  
- safety and hygiene compliance  
- emergency troubleshooting (equipment failures, cold chain issues)  

---

## 4. Operational Problems Solved

### **4.1. Peak-Hour Bottlenecks**
Implemented:
- staffing redistribution,  
- load-balancing between register lanes,  
- shift-overlap optimisation.

### **4.2. Backroom Overflow**
Actions:
- reorganised shelf-stocking sequences,  
- reduced pallet congestion,  
- synchronised unloading with staff availability.

### **4.3. Cold Chain Disruptions**
Ensured:
- rapid restocking of chilled goods,  
- emergency backup handling for refrigeration failures.

### **4.4. Shrinkage Reduction**
Identified:
- high-risk SKUs,  
- procedural loopholes,  
- improper handover practices.

Implemented corrective measures.

## 5. Warehouse Interaction & Replenishment Flow

A retail store cannot operate alone—it depends on upstream logistics.  
Experience included daily coordination with:

- regional distribution centres  
- delivery drivers  
- category managers  
- merchandising teams  
- promotional planners  

Operational tasks required:

- verifying incoming goods  
- resolving missing/damaged items  
- adjusting inventory expectations  
- synchronising store replenishment with warehouse schedules
